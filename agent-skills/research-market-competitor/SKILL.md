# Research: Market & Competitor

Conduct market research, competitor analysis, industry scans, benchmarking, and positioning reviews using reliable up-to-date sources.

## When to Use This Skill

- New market entry or expansion analysis
- Competitor feature comparison
- Pricing strategy research
- Industry trend identification
- Positioning review against alternatives
- Market sizing estimates
- Evidence-backed strategic recommendations

## Research Protocol

### Step 1: Define Scope
1. What market or segment are we analyzing?
2. Which competitors or alternatives matter?
3. What time horizon matters (current vs. emerging)?
4. What decision will this inform?

### Step 2: Gather Sources
Use multiple source types:
- **Primary**: Official documentation, pricing pages, public filings
- **Secondary**: Industry reports, analyst notes, credible publications
- **Tertiary**: User reviews, forum discussions, social sentiment
- **First-party**: Your own product data, user interviews

### Step 3: Analyze
1. **Map the landscape**: Who are the players? What's their positioning?
2. **Compare features**: Head-to-head comparison on key dimensions
3. **Identify patterns**: What recurring strengths/weaknesses emerge?
4. **Find gaps**: Where is the market underserved?
5. **Assess momentum**: Who's growing? Who's losing ground?

### Step 4: Synthesize
Present findings with:
- Clear sourcing for every claim
- Confidence level (high/medium/low) per insight
- Actionable recommendations, not just data
- What's uncertain and why

## Source Quality Guidelines

| Quality | Examples |
|---|---|
| High | Official docs, primary research, verified data |
| Medium | Industry reports, analyst publications |
| Low | Opinion pieces, unverified claims, single sources |

- Prefer high-quality sources. Note when using medium/low.
- Never cite a single source for important claims — triangulate.

## Anti-Patterns
- Don't present opinion as fact
- Don't ignore competitors the user might not know about
- Don't skip the "so what" — every insight needs an action
- Don't use outdated data — note the date of each source
