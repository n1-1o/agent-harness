# Source Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before treating a claim as decision-grade.

## Source Tiers

| Tier | What it includes | How to use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `primary` | Official company pages, filings, earnings calls, product docs, first-party datasets, government data | Default starting point |
| `trusted-secondary` | Analyst reports, reputable publications, respected market databases | Use to validate and add context |
| `weak-signal` | Vendor blogs, affiliate articles, social posts, forums, unverified listicles | Use carefully and label clearly |

## Reliability Checks

For each important source, ask:

1. Who published it?
2. When was it published or last updated?
3. What is the incentive or bias?
4. Is it first-hand or repeated from elsewhere?
5. Can another independent source confirm it?

## Freshness Rules

| Topic | Preferred recency |
| --- | --- |
| AI products, pricing, product launches | Last 3-6 months |
| SaaS competition, positioning, feature sets | Last 6-12 months |
| Mature industries, regulation, infrastructure | Last 12-24 months if still relevant |

If you use older evidence, explain why the claim likely still holds.

## Triangulation Rules

- Strong claims need at least 2 independent sources.
- Market size claims should ideally include a primary or highly trusted source.
- Pricing claims should be checked on the current pricing page when possible.
- Feature claims should be checked against product docs or in-product evidence when possible.

## Bias Checks

Watch for:

- Vendor-funded reports presented as neutral research
- Affiliate content optimized for SEO rather than accuracy
- "Top tools" pages that repeat the same copied claims
- Press releases being reused as independent evidence

## Evidence Ledger

Track important sources in a compact table:

| Source | Tier | Date | Claim supported | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Name or link] | `primary` | YYYY-MM-DD | [Claim] | [Bias, caveat, or gap] |

## Confidence Guidance

| Confidence | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `high` | Multiple strong, recent, independent sources align |
| `medium` | Evidence is good but incomplete, dated, or partly inferred |
| `low` | Evidence is thin, conflicting, or weak-signal only |

## Contradictions

If sources disagree:

- State the disagreement plainly.
- Do not merge conflicting claims into one neat sentence.
- Explain which source is more credible and why.
- Note what additional evidence would resolve the conflict.
